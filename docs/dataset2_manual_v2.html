Dataset 2 is a four-account productive capital dataset built from the current BEA vintage (September 2025 revision). It is distinct from Dataset 1 — the sealed Shaikh replication using the 2011 BEA vintage (1947–2011). The two datasets must never be mixed in estimation.

The dataset provides the capital stock series for robustness analysis of the θ(Λ) estimation in S3 and for the Chile comparative axis. It extends the GPIM capital stock construction to a theoretically correct perimeter: nonfinancial corporate business plus government transportation infrastructure, under a Weibull retirement distribution with a cold-start initialization consistent with the unbalanced growth framework.

Relationship to Dataset 1 Dataset 1 uses Shaikh's original corporate perimeter (total corporate, GVA output, L/2 Weibull initialization, 2011 BEA vintage). Dataset 2 uses a theoretically motivated narrower perimeter (NF corporate + gov transport, NVA output, cold-start Weibull, 2025 BEA vintage). Results are not directly comparable — the vintage gap and accounting differences are documented findings, not bugs.

§5

Capital Perimeter

The productive capital perimeter follows the Basu (2022) circuit-capital framework, not Shaikh's broader "social capital" argument (Shaikh 2016, Ch. 7). The distinction matters for what belongs to node P versus node X in the circuit N→P→C′.

NF corporate — why not total corporate

FAAt601 (Section 6, legal form only) distinguishes Financial corporate (L3) from Nonfinancial corporate (L4). Financial corporate capital (bank branches, data centers, trading floors) enables the circulation of money capital — it belongs to the M–M′ circuit of financial capital, not to the M–C...P...C′–M′ circuit of productive capital. Including it would contaminate the productive capital stock with circulation-enabling assets.

Government transportation — theoretical grounding

The inclusion of government transportation rests on Marx Vol. II, Ch. 6:

Marx Vol. II Ch. 6 "The productive capital invested in [transport] creates value and surplus-value... The change of place of the commodities is the use-value of the means of transport."

Transport adds locational use-value to commodities as part of the production process — before the commodity enters exchange. The P→C′ boundary is the handoff to the retailer for sale, not the mode or medium of transport. Therefore all transport infrastructure (road, rail, air, water) belongs to node P regardless of ownership (public/private).

Hazard function

Weibull hazard — instantaneous retirement rate at age τ \[ h(\tau) = \frac{\alpha}{\lambda}\left(\frac{\tau}{\lambda}\right)^{\alpha-1} \qquad \text{where } \lambda = \frac{L}{\Gamma(1+\alpha^{-1})} \]

GPIM Accumulation Rules

The Generalized Perpetual Inventory Method (Shaikh 2016, Appendix 6.5) derives the aggregate accumulation rule that survives Fisher-ideal chain aggregation — closing the gap that the standard micro-level PIM leaves open when assets are chain-weighted.

Core rules

Current-cost gross stock \[ K_t = IG_t + z_t^* \cdot K_{t-1} \qquad z_t^* \equiv (1-z_t)\,\frac{p_t^K}{p_{t-1}^K} \]
Constant-cost (real) gross stock \[ K_t^R = IG_t^R + (1-\rho_t)\,K_{t-1}^R \qquad IG_t^R \equiv IG_t / p_t^K \]
Theoretically correct aggregate depreciation rate (ADJ1) \[ z_t = \frac{D_t}{p_t^K \cdot K_{t-1}^R} \]
Uses reflated lagged real stock as denominator. Whelan-Liu approximation (D_t / K_{t-1}) is available as sensitivity only via USE_SHAIKH_BEA1993_RATES toggle.

SFC identity

Stock-flow consistency — enforced at every step \[ K_t^{NC} - K_{t-1}^{NC} \equiv IG_t - D_t \]
Internal tolerance: 1e-6 (floating-point machine epsilon below this). Gate 1 tolerance: 1e-4. Pipeline halts if violated.

Mean-age recursion (time-varying ρ)

Investment-weighted mean age at t+1 \[ \bar\tau_{t+1} = \frac{\bar\tau_t \cdot (1-\rho_t) \cdot K_t^{GR}}{(1-\rho_t)\cdot K_t^{GR} + IG_t^R} + 1 \]
Survivors age 1 year; new investment enters at age 0. ρ_t = h(τ̄_t) is recomputed annually. No balanced-growth assumption.

Cold-Start Warmup

The standard initialization τ̄₀ = L/2 asserts a balanced-growth ergodic vintage distribution — the stock is already in steady state under constant investment growth. This is precisely the closure the unbalanced growth framework rejects.

Correct initialization under unbalanced growth

Cold start conditions at 1901 \[ \bar\tau_{1901} = 0 \qquad K^{GR}_{1901} = 0 \]

The mean-age recursion runs from 1901–1924, driven entirely by actual BEA investment flows. warmup_from_investment() returns both K_R_terminal and tau_bar_terminal. Post-warmup recursion initializes from tau_bar_1925.

§9

p_lag Boundary Assumption

The current-cost recursion requires p^K_{t−1} at the first observation year (1925). BEA chain-type quantity indexes begin in 1925, so p^K_{1924} does not exist in the data. The pipeline sets:

Boundary condition — imposed by data availability \[ p_{1924}^K = p_{1925}^K \]

This implies zero price change at the 1924–1925 boundary. It is not an equilibrium assumption — it is a data availability constraint.

Error propagation under unbalanced growth

Initialization error propagation \[ \Delta K_t = \Delta K_0 \cdot \prod_{s=1}^{t} z_s^* \]

The error contracts each period by z*_s. Since z*_s < 1 whenever z_s > g_{p^K,s}/(1 + g_{p^K,s}) — confirmed for the US corporate sector — the error converges to zero. No constant convergence rate can be stated without imposing balanced growth. The contraction rate is time-varying, governed by the historical z*_t sequence. The error is negligible within the estimation window (1947–2024).

§18

Government Transport: Theoretical Status

Government transportation capital was removed from KGC_productive on 2026-03-28. This resolves a valuation mismatch that was causing Gate 2 to check SFC across two incompatible accounting systems.

The mismatch

The GPIM SFC identity K_t - K_{t-1} ≡ IG_t - D_t holds within a single accounting perimeter. For NF corporate capital: KNC comes from FAAt601 (private books), IG from FAAt607 (private investment), D from NIPA T1.14 L18 (corporate CCA). Government transport capital lives in a different system: FAAt701 (government books), FAAt705 (government investment), government CCA — none of which enters NF corporate income accounts.

The resolution

KGC_productive = KGC_NF_corp only (locked decision 2026-03-28) Government transportation infrastructure enters the capacity function as a productivity shifter — an external conditioning variable that raises the productivity of private productive capital without being capital at node P. This follows the Basu (2022) circuit-capital framework: public infrastructure enables the circuit N→P→C′ as a general condition of production, but it is not capital on the books of the NF corporate sector whose output (NVA_NF) we are measuring.

How gov_trans enters modelling

KGC_gov_trans is retained in kstock_master.csv as an auxiliary column. In the capacity function it enters as:

Two-input capacity function (for modelling) \[ Y_t^p = A_0 e^{bt} \cdot ( ext{KGC\_NF\_corp}_t)^{ heta_1} \cdot f( ext{KGC\_gov\_trans}_t) \cdot / p_t \]
Where f(·) is a productivity-shift function — log-linear under constant returns: θ₂·log(KGC_gov_trans). Alternatively KGC_gov_trans enters as a level-shift variable with θ₂ = 1.
Open: theoretical grounding of gov_trans as productivity shifter The two-input capacity function requires separate identification of θ₁ and θ₂. The single-θ ARDL cannot recover both. This is a future extension for the Chile comparative axis where gov_trans variation is more pronounced.
